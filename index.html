About Me

Know Me More

Student

Having played the bass from the age of 9, Trey has had several personal instructors over the years and has been in circles of music academia since high school. Learning formally under producer and bassist Mark Peterson, a NJ/NY-based player who himself was a student of Stanley Clarke, Trey got pushed into mature scenes of music at the age of just 16.

He is currently taking lessons with a double-bass instructor and planning to enter grad school for music this upcoming year all while working full-time as a bassist. His eagerness to continually learn even with his current successes set him apart.

Educator

Trey has used his formal and informal education on the instrument and stage to influence many young musicians. Having his own private teaching studio in his home, and working through lesson centers in local music stores, he has quite a few years of teaching under his belt for how young he is.

He served as a musical director at a congregate in his current residence of Springfield, MO. He has been invited to do bass clinics across his city as well. His biggest influences in his playing are Marcus Miller, Jaco Pastorius, James Jamerson, and Sharay Reed.
