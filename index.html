Get To Know Me

Biography

An adept and versatile bass player, Trey Sederwall hails from the vibrant music scenes of New Jersey and New York. With roots in R&B, gospel music, and jazz, Trey's range of talent as a bass player has made him a sought-after accompanist for numerous artists over the years.

With a natural aptitude for the instrument and an unwavering commitment to honing his craft, Trey's talents have quickly caught the attention of fellow musicians and industry professionals. Playing on stages and in studios providing the foundation for other artists, Trey has now taken a step into the spotlight with his own music. Drawing inspiration from his diverse backgrounds, Trey weaves together a project bringing some jazz, some blues, and a little funk to create a unique sound that musical listeners can appreciate.

As a dedicated musician and lifelong student of music, Trey Sederwall continues to evolve as an artist, always seeking new avenues for self-expression and growth. With his undeniable talent, rich musical heritage, and unwavering passion, Trey Sederwall is poised to make a lasting impact on the world of music and beyond.

Student

Having played the bass from the age of 9, Trey has had several personal instructors over the years and has been in circles of music academia since high school. Learning formally under producer and bassist Mark Peterson, a NJ/NY-based player who himself was a student of Stanley Clarke, Trey got pushed into mature scenes of music at the age of just 16.

He is currently taking lessons with a double-bass instructor and planning to enter grad school for music this upcoming year all while working full-time as a bassist.

Educator

Trey has used his formal and informal education on the instrument and stage to influence many young musicians. Having his own private teaching studio in his home, and working through lesson centers in local music stores, he has quite a few years of teaching under his belt for how young he is.

He served as a musical director at a congregate in his current residence of Springfield, MO. He has been invited to do bass clinics across his city as well. His biggest influences in his playing are Marcus Miller, Jaco Pastorius, James Jamerson, and Sharay Reed.
